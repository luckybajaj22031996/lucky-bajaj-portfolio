Smart Food Label Checker
AI ¬∑ Consumer Health ¬∑ Personal Project ¬∑ 0‚Üí1
Problem Statement

Nutrition labels are information-heavy but decision-poor. Consumers face a wall of numbers ‚Äî calories, macros, ingredient lists ‚Äî with no clear signal on whether a product is suitable for them. The gap isn't data; it's interpretation. Even health-conscious shoppers hesitate at the shelf because translating nutritional data into a personal judgement call requires knowledge most people don't have.

Solution & How It Works

Smart Food Label Checker uses computer vision + AI reasoning to extract nutritional data from a label photo and deliver a structured health verdict in under 5 seconds.

Key Design Decisions & Tradeoffs

Prompt-based scoring over rigid rules: Rather than fixed thresholds (e.g. "sugar > 10g = bad"), the product uses AI-guided reasoning ‚Äî enabling nuanced trade-offs like high fat but high protein in a nut bar. This sacrifices some consistency for realistic, contextual judgement.

Mobile-first, minimal inputs: Deliberate choice to require zero mandatory inputs at launch. Personalization is opt-in. This reduces friction at first use, with the tradeoff of less tailored early scores ‚Äî acceptable given the discovery goal.

Category-awareness over universal scoring: A 5g sugar biscuit is very different from a 5g sugar fruit ‚Äî context-aware judgement prevents misleading verdicts, though it adds prompt complexity.

Roadmap
  • Phase 1 (MVP): Image upload ‚Üí AI extraction ‚Üí health score + verdict display
  • Phase 2: Barcode scanning, alternative product suggestions
  • Phase 3: Grocery basket health analysis, dietary pattern tracking
  • Phase 4: Integration with fitness apps (MyFitnessPal, Apple Health)

Commute Optimization System
Data Engineering ¬∑ Personal Analytics ¬∑ Powai ‚Üî Andheri, Mumbai
Problem Statement

My Powai ‚Üî Andheri commute varied by 30‚Äì40 minutes depending on departure time, yet every decision was based on intuition. Without historical data, it was impossible to distinguish real traffic patterns from perceived randomness ‚Äî and every suboptimal departure meant avoidable time lost.

User Personas

Lucky ‚Äî Primary User

Urban Commuter ‚Äî Extensible
Any high-traffic city resident
Reusable framework for any route with significant travel-time variability
"Google Maps tells me current duration, not the best time to leave."
Hypothesis

"If I collect enough timestamped travel duration data across different departure windows, I can identify statistically reliable patterns that reveal an optimal departure window ‚Äî reducing average commute time without changing the route."

Solution Architecture
1
Data Acquisition
Google Maps Directions API ‚Äî queried duration_in_traffic with live departure timestamps for both route directions
2
Automation Pipeline
Python script fetches at fixed intervals, converts to IST, appends to CSV ‚Äî runs via GitHub Actions (cloud) and cron jobs (local) with zero manual intervention
3
Analysis Layer
Trend visualization, time-window filtering, peak vs. off-peak comparison, and directional pattern analysis
4
Decision Output
Identify departure windows with consistently lowest travel duration ‚Äî targeted for eventual push alerts

Key Learnings
  • Traffic variability is quantifiable: Duration swings were larger and more predictable than intuition suggested
  • Directionality matters: Powai‚ÜíAndheri and Andheri‚ÜíPowai have meaningfully different congestion fingerprints by hour
  • Small datasets mislead: Early patterns required weeks of validation before acting ‚Äî a core PM lesson about premature conclusions
  • Automation removes behavioural bias: Scheduled collection removes the human tendency to only check when it feels convenient
